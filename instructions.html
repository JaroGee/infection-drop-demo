THE INFECTION
GAME. Working Prototype · How to Use

A plain-language guide to what this prototype is, why it exists, and how to run a demo. Written for anyone seeing this for the first time.

01 · WHAT IS THIS

A game about peer pressure.

This is a phone-based game designed to make young people feel — not just hear about — how peer pressure works.

Young people often make risky decisions not because they want to, but because everyone around them seems to be doing it. That social pressure is hard to see and even harder to resist. This game makes it visible.

Players join a small group called a cell — think of it like a friend group. Every day for seven days, they receive a short story about a pressure situation a teenager might actually face. Then they make a choice: Hold (stay firm with their group) or Release (give in for a personal advantage).

The twist: every player can see what the rest of their group is doing, in real time, on their own phone. When someone gives in, the infection meter on every phone goes up. When people hold firm, it goes down. The group's choices are no longer invisible — they are live, on screen, felt by everyone.

THE METAPHOR
Peer pressure as infection

When one person gives in, it makes it easier for others to follow. The "infection" spreads through a group the same way risky behaviour does — quietly, socially, one decision at a time.

THE INSIGHT
Most people want to hold

Research shows most young people do not actually want to engage in risky behaviour — they just feel alone in that position. Seeing your group hold firm in real time gives you the social backing to do the same.

02 · WHY IT EXISTS

Part of a larger campaign.

This prototype is built for NCADA's Silent Majority pilot — a street campaign and live event designed to shift how young people in Singapore think about drug use and peer influence.

The campaign runs over seven days on the streets around *SCAPE Somerset, a youth hub in Orchard Road. Young people encounter it on the street, join a cell with their friends, and play the daily game on their phones as the week progresses.

On the final Saturday, every player's week of decisions feeds into a live broadcast moment at *SCAPE — The Reckoning — where the collective result of the week is revealed on screen to everyone in the room. The campaign ends with a structured conversation and a direct link to support resources.

The goal is not to lecture. It is to give young people the experience of holding firm together, so they know what that feels like before they face real pressure.

03 · HOW TO RUN A DEMO

Six steps, two phones.

Best experienced with two people side by side, each on their own phone. One phone works too for a solo walkthrough. The whole demo takes under five minutes.

  1. Open the link on your phone
    Visit infection-drop-demo.vercel.app in any mobile browser — Safari, Chrome, anything. No app to download. No account to create. Just open the link.
  2. Choose your initial
    Type one letter — your initial. This is how the rest of the cell will see you on screen. If two people are demoing together, use different letters so you can tell yourselves apart.
  3. Enter the cell code
    Both phones must type the same code to join the same cell. Use DEMO for a shared demo cell, or make up any word you like. The code is what links two phones together.
  4. Read the day's story and make your choice
    Each day opens with a short scenario — a moment of social pressure a teenager might recognise. Tap HOLD to stay firm with the group, or RELEASE to go it alone. Watch the other phone — when your partner decides, their initial changes colour on your screen and the infection meter responds immediately.
  5. Advance through the days
    Tap Advance Day at the bottom to move to the next day. There are seven days in total. After Day 7, both phones arrive at The Reckoning — a summary of the week's decisions across the whole cell.
  6. Reset for the next group
    On The Reckoning screen, tap Replay Demo. This wipes all decisions, removes all players, and resets the cell back to Day 1 — completely clean for the next group. No extra steps, no URL, no manual cleanup needed.
04 · WHAT TO POINT OUT

Five things to show your audience.

If you are demonstrating this to someone who has not seen it before, these are the moments worth highlighting.

  1. The story makes the choice feel real
    The daily scenario is not a survey question. It is a situation — a friend group chat, someone waiting at the bike rack after school, a photo circulating overnight. Young people recognise these moments. That recognition is the point.
  2. One phone updates the other instantly
    When one player makes their choice, the other phone updates within a fraction of a second — no refresh, no waiting. Show this live. It is the most visceral part of the demo.
  3. The infection meter is the group, not the individual
    Point out that the percentage on screen belongs to the whole cell, not any one player. Individual decisions feed a shared outcome. That is the campaign's core idea made visible.
  4. The member dots tell the social story at a glance
    Red filled dot: that player held. Grey dot: they released. Faded: not yet decided. In five seconds, anyone can read the social state of the entire group without a word being said.
  5. The Reckoning is the campaign's climax
    In the full live event at *SCAPE Somerset, The Reckoning is a broadcast moment — the week's results shown on screen to an audience of hundreds. This prototype gives you a feel for how that moment lands at a small scale.
05 · PRODUCTION ROADMAP

What the full pilot adds on top of this.

This prototype covers the player-side daily loop only. The full pilot build extends this into a complete campaign infrastructure.

  • VERIFIED SIGN-IN
    Phone number sign-in with consent capture, replacing the demo's open cell-code model. Every player is verified, every consent record is retained, age-gating is applied where required.
  • CONTENT MODERATION
    AI moderation with human oversight for all participant content. Sensitive content is escalated; final decisions remain with the human team.
  • THE CONFESSIONAL
    A physical voice station on the street where passersby can anonymously record a short voice message. Recordings are modulated, moderated, and fed into the Saturday broadcast.
  • THE RECKONING — LIVE VOTE
    The anchoring vote moment at the live event. Every phone in the room and every online viewer responds simultaneously, with instant aggregation shown on the broadcast screen.
  • THE CONVERSATION INTERFACE
    The closing segment of the live event. A structured conversation flow, exit pathways, support resource handoff, and post-event survey aligned to NCADA's HIGH evaluation framework.
  • STREET INTEGRATION
    The plaza interactive at *SCAPE Somerset, the street-to-phone recruitment flow, and on-site booth operations. Sub-200ms input-to-display response under live operating conditions.
  • REPORTING & EVALUATION
    Pre and post measurement informed by NCADA's HIGH methodology. Operational dashboards, nightly digests, and a final NCADA-ready evaluation report at the close of the pilot.
A NOTE ON THIS PROTOTYPE

This is a working demo, not the finished product. The content is illustrative, there is no moderation, and anyone can join with any cell code. It is built to show the core mechanic in your hand — not to simulate the full campaign. Everything described in the roadmap above is part of the pilot proposal and is not yet built.
