THE INFECTION
GAME. Working Prototype · How to Use

A working slice of the player-side daily loop, built to make the campaign mechanic tangible. Two phones, one cell, and the seven-day decision rhythm the proposal describes — running live, on real devices, in your hand.

01 · WHAT THE PROTOTYPE PROVES

The mechanic, in your hand.

The prototype demonstrates the campaign's central thesis: that peer pressure is most visible when it is shared in real time. Two people in the same room, looking at the same cell, watching each other's choices ripple across both screens. The proposition the proposal argues becomes something you can feel inside a minute.

WHAT IT SHOWS
The daily loop

Story drop, pressure decision, cell status update, and the choice carrying forward into the next day. The 60-second rhythm at the heart of the seven-day campaign.

WHAT IT PROVES
Real-time sync

When one player makes a decision, the other player's screen updates within a fraction of a second. The peer mechanic is not a metaphor. It is the product.

02 · HOW TO USE IT

Six steps, two phones.

Best experienced with two people side by side, each on their own phone. One phone works too if you would like a solo walkthrough.

  1. Open the link on your phone
    Visit infection-drop-demo.vercel.app in any mobile browser. No app to download. No account to create.
  2. Choose your initial
    One letter. The cell will see you only by this letter. For two-person demos, pick different letters so you can tell yourselves apart on screen.
  3. Enter the cell code
    Both phones type the same code. Anything works — NCADA, SCAPE, the date, a word you both agree on. Typing the same code puts you in the same cell.
  4. Read the day's story and make your choice
    Each day opens with a short pressure scenario. HOLD stays with the cell. RELEASE claims a solo advantage. Watch the other phone — when your partner decides, their initial on your screen changes colour, and the infection meter responds.
  5. Advance the days
    Tap Advance Day at the bottom to move forward. Seven days total. After Day 7, both phones land on The Reckoning — a final status reflecting the week's decisions across the cell.
  6. Reset the demo
    On The Reckoning screen, tap Replay Demo. This wipes all decisions, removes all players, and resets the cell back to Day 1 — ready for the next group. No URL or extra steps needed.
03 · WHAT YOU WILL SEE

Five things to notice.

  1. The story changes the room
    Each day opens with a short scenario — a moment of pressure, a phone buzzing, a friend asking. The decision lands inside a story, not a survey.
  2. Decisions appear instantly on both phones
    The other phone updates within a fraction of a second. No refresh, no waiting. This is the same technical foundation the live campaign will use.
  3. The infection meter moves with the cell
    More HOLD decisions push the meter down. More RELEASE decisions push it up. The cell is the unit. Individual choices feed a collective state.
  4. Member pills tell the social story
    Filled red means the player held. Grey means they released. Faded means they have not yet decided today. The cell's social state, at a glance.
  5. The Reckoning closes the week
    On Day 7, the prototype closes with a reflection on what the cell achieved together. In the full campaign, this is the live broadcast moment at *SCAPE Somerset.
04 · PRODUCTION ROADMAP

What the pilot delivers on top of this.

The prototype is the player-side daily loop. The full pilot build extends this foundation across the campaign architecture described in the proposal.

  • PRODUCTION AUTHENTICATION
    Phone-OTP sign-in with PDPA-aligned consent capture, replacing the demo's open-access cell-code model. Verified players, retained consent records, age-gating where required.
  • CONTENT MODERATION
    AI moderation layered with human review for all participant-generated content. Tone safety, sensitive-content escalation, and final-decision authority retained under human judgement.
  • THE CONFESSIONAL
    The static voice station that runs across all six street days. Anonymous voice capture with modulation, ingestion into the moderation queue, and content flow into the Saturday broadcast.
  • THE RECKONING VOTE INFRASTRUCTURE
    The single anchoring vote moment under live audience load. Every phone in the room and every viewer online responding to the same question simultaneously, with instant aggregation and broadcast-ready graphics.
  • THE CONVERSATION INTERFACE
    The closing-act stage flow. NCADA's airtime, structured exit pathways, support-resource handoff, and post-event survey capture aligned to the HIGH-informed evaluation framework.
  • STREET-LAYER INTEGRATION
    The plaza interactive at *SCAPE Somerset, the booth conversion flow, and the daily loop's integration with on-site recruitment. Sub-200ms input-to-display latency under tested operating conditions.
  • REPORTING & EVALUATION
    Pre and post measurement informed by NCADA's HIGH methodology. Operational dashboards for the production team, nightly digests, and final NCADA-ready report at the close of the pilot.
A NOTE ON THIS PROTOTYPE

This is a working prototype, not a production system. Content is illustrative, moderation is intentionally absent, and access is open by cell code rather than verified identity. Treat it as a proof of mechanic. The pilot's full production scope is in the proposal.
